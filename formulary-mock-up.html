Frequently Asked Questions · 7 questions

Is Ozempic covered by Marketplace plans in North Carolina?
Yes. Ozempic (semaglutide injection) is covered on 47 of 52 ACA Marketplace plans available in North Carolina for the 2026 plan year. It is most commonly placed on the Preferred Brand (Tier 2) formulary, with a typical post-deductible copay of $30–$60 per fill. Coverage is generally for the Type 2 diabetes indication; weight-loss use may require separate prior authorization under Wegovy.
Does Ozempic require prior authorization?
Yes, prior authorization is required on 38 of 47 plans. Your doctor submits a request with your diagnosis code and clinical rationale, and the insurer must respond within 2–3 business days (24–72 hours for urgent cases). Most properly documented PA requests are approved. If denied, you have the right to a peer-to-peer review and formal appeal.
How much does Ozempic cost with insurance?
Ozempic typically costs $30–$60 per fill on most NC Marketplace plans as a preferred brand drug. However, this is the post-deductible copay — if you haven't met your annual deductible, you'll pay the plan's negotiated rate ($800–$1,000 per pen). A 90-day mail-order supply often saves roughly 30% compared to three monthly fills.
What tier is Ozempic on?
Ozempic is placed on a Preferred Brand (Tier 2) tier on most NC Marketplace plans. This means moderate cost-sharing. Tier placement varies by insurer — the same drug can be Tier 2 on one plan and Tier 3 on another, depending on PBM rebate contracts. This is one of the most important reasons to check the specific formulary of any plan before enrolling.
What if Ozempic is not covered by my plan?
You have three paths. First, request a formulary exception — your doctor submits a letter of medical necessity. Second, file a formal internal appeal (success rate ~40–50% when documented). Third, request an independent External Review Organization (IRO) review under 45 CFR § 147.136 — the IRO decision is binding on the insurer. Simultaneously, ask about formulary-covered alternatives like Trulicity or Rybelsus.
Can I switch plans to get Ozempic covered?
Yes, during Open Enrollment (Nov 1 – Jan 15) or a qualifying Special Enrollment Period. If your plan stops covering Ozempic mid-year, that may qualify as an SEP. Always verify the new plan's formulary before enrolling — formularies change every January 1.
What's the difference between a formulary exception and prior authorization?
Prior authorization requires your doctor to document medical necessity before your insurer will cover a drug — it's about activating coverage. A formulary exception asks the insurer to cover a drug at a lower tier or cover an off-formulary drug — it's about reducing cost. Both require documentation; they solve different problems. You can file both simultaneously.
