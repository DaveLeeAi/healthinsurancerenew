## 15. SECTION: WHEN COMPARING PLANS FOR OZEMPIC, CHECK THESE IN ORDER

**WHEN COMPARING PLANS FOR OZEMPIC, CHECK THESE IN ORDER**

**1. Is Ozempic on your plan's drug list?**
Every South Carolina marketplace plan for 2026 lists Ozempic — all 125 of them. Still, confirm your specific plan's drug list before you enroll, as coverage terms and tiers can change.

**2. What tier is it on?**
The tier determines what you pay after your deductible. All six South Carolina insurance companies place Ozempic on their lower-cost brand tier. What you pay each month still depends on the plan — check the Ozempic copay before you enroll.

**3. Does your deductible apply to prescriptions?**
Most plans require you to meet a deductible before the lower copay kicks in. Some plans have a separate, smaller deductible just for prescriptions — which means you reach the lower copay sooner. Others combine drug and medical costs into one deductible, so you may pay the pre-deductible price longer.

**4. Does your plan require approval before you can fill it?**
Most South Carolina marketplace plans require approval before you can fill Ozempic (called prior authorization), but a few do not. Approval can delay your first fill on the plans that require it. If you are denied, you can appeal.

If you take Ozempic, a plan with a lower drug tier and a lower deductible will usually cost you less over the year — even if the monthly premium is slightly higher.

**WHY OZEMPIC COSTS MORE TO COVER THAN MANY DRUGS**

Ozempic is a brand-name injectable, not a generic. ## 19. SECTION: IF IT IS TAKING TOO LONG OR YOUR PLAN DENIES COVERAGE

**IF IT IS TAKING TOO LONG OR YOUR PLAN DENIES COVERAGE**

**1. Call your doctor's office for an update**
If you have been waiting longer than a few days and have not heard anything, call your doctor's office. They may be able to tell you whether the approval is still pending.

**2. If denied, your doctor may be able to ask for another review directly**
Your doctor may be able to ask for another review directly. This is sometimes called a peer-to-peer review. It is not offered by every plan, so ask your doctor's office early whether this is an option.

**3. File a formal appeal — or switch plans at the next open enrollment**
If the denial stands, your plan will send you a notice explaining why and how to appeal. Check that notice closely — it will include the deadline and the steps to follow. If Ozempic coverage continues to be a problem, open enrollment is your chance to switch to a different plan.

## 23. FAQ SECTION

**COMMON QUESTIONS ABOUT OZEMPIC COVERAGE IN SOUTH CAROLINA**

**Q: Can I switch plans mid-year to get better Ozempic coverage?**
In most cases, no. You can only switch mid-year if you have a major life change — like losing other coverage, getting married, or having a baby. If you are planning to switch at the next open enrollment, check the new plan's Ozempic tier, whether it requires approval, and what you would likely pay after your deductible.

**Q: What is the difference between prior authorization and a coverage exception?**
Prior authorization is approval your plan requires before it will cover a drug that is on its drug list. A coverage exception is a request to cover a drug that is not on your plan's drug list, or to move it to a lower-cost tier. Both are filed by your doctor with documentation. The plan reviews each one against its own rules.

**Q: Will my plan tell me if Ozempic's coverage changes during the year?**
Plans send a written notice if they remove a drug, change its tier, or add new approval rules during the plan year. The notice usually arrives by mail or through your plan's member portal. If you take Ozempic regularly, watch for these notices and check your plan's drug list a few weeks before each refill.

**Q: If Ozempic is denied, is Wegovy a covered alternative?**
Wegovy and Ozempic share the same active ingredient but are approved for different uses — Ozempic for type 2 diabetes, Wegovy for weight management. Coverage rules differ. Check your plan's drug list for Wegovy specifically before assuming it is an alternative. See how Wegovy is covered in South Carolina.

**Q: Does every Affordable Care Act (ACA) plan in South Carolina have to cover Ozempic?**
No. The ACA requires plans to cover prescription drugs as an essential health benefit, but each plan decides which specific drugs to include on its drug list. All 125 South Carolina marketplace plans for 2026 happen to cover Ozempic, but coverage is not federally required for any specific brand-name drug.

**Q: Can Ozempic coverage change after I enroll in a plan?**
Yes. Plans can update their drug lists during the plan year. They can move a drug to a different tier, add prior authorization requirements, or remove a drug entirely. Your plan must give written notice before most changes take effect. Once you are enrolled, you generally cannot switch plans until the next open enrollment unless you have a qualifying life event.
