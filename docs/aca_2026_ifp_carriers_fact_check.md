# 2026 IFP ACA Carrier Fact Check — 50 States + DC

Last updated: 2026-04-04

## Scope
This file is a practical fact-check reference for **2026 individual/family ACA Marketplace carriers** (on-exchange/QHP individual market) for all 50 states plus DC.

## Method
- Marketplace type cross-check: CMS and HealthCare.gov marketplace references.
- Carrier lists: state-level 2026 marketplace guides on HealthInsurance.org, which in turn cite state marketplaces, state DOI/SERFF/rate-review filings, and CMS open-enrollment/public-use files.
- Where a page did **not** print the full carrier bullet list cleanly, I inferred the continuing 2026 carrier list from that same page’s **2026 approved-rate table**.

## Important caveats
- This is a **carrier roster fact check**, not a county-level service-area file. Some carriers are available only in parts of a state.
- A few source pages contain internal inconsistencies. I flagged the biggest ones below.
- “Carrier” sometimes reflects a legal entity, sometimes a brand/subsidiary, depending on how the state/page reports it.

## Notable inconsistencies flagged
- **California:** the source says 11 carriers for 2026 but still shows **Aetna CVS Health** in the bullet list while also saying Aetna exited at the end of 2025. I treated **Aetna as exited** and excluded it from the 2026 count.
- **Georgia:** quick-facts area shows **10** carriers, but the detailed 2026 carrier section lists **8** carriers and specifically notes Aetna exited. I treated the detailed carrier section as the better source and used **8**.

## High-level takeaways
- The 2026 ACA individual market saw a meaningful number of **Aetna exits**, affecting multiple states.
- Several states had **new entrants** or renamed/shifted legal entities rather than a clean count increase.
- State-based marketplaces and federally facilitated states both experienced noticeable churn, so a 2025 list should **not** be assumed to remain correct for 2026.
