## What Determines Health Insurance Premiums?

Health insurance premiums on the ACA marketplace are determined by five primary factors: age, geographic location, tobacco use, plan metal tier, and household income (through subsidies). Unlike pre-ACA markets, current regulations prohibit insurers from adjusting premiums based on health status or gender.

## How Age Influences Cost

Age is the most significant individual rating factor for ACA premiums. Older adults are charged higher premiums than younger adults, with federal rules allowing insurers to charge up to three times more for the oldest age band compared to the youngest. This age-based pricing reflects the statistical increase in healthcare utilization as people age.

## The Role of Geographic Location

Geographic rating areas drive meaningful premium differences even within the same state. Areas with higher healthcare delivery costs, fewer competing insurers, or greater utilization patterns typically have higher premiums. A plan that costs one amount in a metropolitan area may carry a substantially different price in a rural county within the same state.

## Tobacco Use Surcharges

Insurers are permitted to apply a tobacco surcharge of up to 50% on premiums for individuals who use tobacco products. Importantly, premium tax credits do not offset the surcharge portion of the premium, meaning the additional cost falls entirely on the enrollee.

## Plan Metal Tier and Out-of-Pocket Balance

Selecting a metal tier is a direct trade-off between monthly premiums and cost-sharing at the point of care. Bronze plans offer the lowest premiums but require the enrollee to pay a larger share of costs through deductibles and copays. Platinum plans minimize out-of-pocket expenses but carry the highest monthly premiums. Evaluating expected healthcare needs for the year helps determine which tier offers the best overall value.
