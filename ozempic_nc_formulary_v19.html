Common questions
Is Ozempic covered by North Carolina health plans in 2026? ▼
Ozempic was listed on 47 of the 52 North Carolina health plans we reviewed for 2026. Five plans did not include it. Whether the plan you're considering covers it depends on that specific plan — worth verifying before you enroll.
How much will Ozempic cost me before I meet my deductible? ▼
Before you've hit your deductible, you pay your plan's negotiated rate — not the full retail price, but still a significant amount. In the plans we reviewed, this typically fell between $400 and $650 per month. Novo Nordisk offers a savings card for commercially insured patients that may help reduce this cost — ask your doctor's office or pharmacist for current eligibility details.
Will I need approval from my insurance before picking up Ozempic? ▼
In our review, 38 of the 47 NC plans that covered Ozempic required prior authorization before the prescription could be filled. Your doctor's office typically handles the paperwork — they submit your diagnosis and supporting clinical information to the plan. Whether a request is approved depends on your plan's criteria and your specific clinical situation. Check your benefit documents for the timeline that applies to your plan.
What tier does Ozempic fall under in North Carolina plans? ▼
Most plans we reviewed placed Ozempic on a preferred brand tier — 35 of the 47 that covered it. Twelve plans put it on a non-preferred tier, which typically means a higher monthly copay. The tier your plan assigns has a direct impact on what you pay after meeting your deductible, so it's one of the most important things to compare when choosing a plan.
What if my plan doesn't cover Ozempic? ▼
You have a few options. You can ask your plan for a coverage exception — your doctor submits documentation explaining why this specific drug is needed. If that's denied, you can appeal. Or, if it's open enrollment or you qualify for a special enrollment period, you can switch to a plan that does cover it. See all NC drug coverage options to compare plans.
Can I switch plans to get Ozempic covered in North Carolina? ▼
You can switch during open enrollment (November 1 through January 15 in NC) or a qualifying special enrollment period. Drug lists are updated each January, so verify that Ozempic is included in any new plan before you commit to it.
What's the difference between a coverage exception and prior approval? ▼
Prior approval (prior authorization) is a step your doctor takes before filling a prescription that's already on your plan's drug list — the plan checks that clinical criteria are met. A coverage exception is a request to have a drug added to your coverage that isn't currently on your plan's list, or to get it placed at a lower cost tier. Both require your doctor's documentation.
